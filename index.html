TERMS AND CONDITIONS

1. THE CONTRACT
This Certificate, the Schedule and any endorsement in the Certificate or the Schedule shall be read together as one contract and any word or expression to which specific meaning has been attached to any part of this Certificate or the Schedule shall bear such specific meaning wherever it may appear. All statements made by the Participant or any Person Covered shall in the absence of fraud be deemed representations and not warranties and no such statement shall void this Certificate or be used in defend of a claim.

2. SHARI'AH COMPLIANCE
The Group Family Takaful complies with the Advisory Council of Experts (ACE) terms of reference of Salam Takaful Insurance Co. Ltd having reviewed the Group Family Takaful product structure, contract document, product literature and associated processes that are in accordance with principles of Shari'ah.

3. DEFINITION
It is expressly declared and agreed between the Takaful Operator and the Participant that for the Purpose of this Certificate:
"Policy Year" means the period of one year from the Commencement Date or any Renewal Date.
"Renewal Date" means any subsequent anniversary of the Commencement Date.
"Service" means service with the Participant, excluding any service in which an employee would not be eligible under the Certificate.
"Employee" means a person in the regular full-time service of the Participant.
"Eligible Employee" means an Employee who qualifies to be included in the Certificate having fulfilled all necessary conditions of employment.
"Persons Covered" means an Eligible Employee who is included in this Certificate and the Certificate depends of their lives.
"Scheme" means the Group Family Takaful Plan of the Participant.

4. CONDITIONS PRECEDENT
The due and fulfilment of the terms, conditions and endorsements of the Certificate in so far as they relate to anything to be done or complied with by the Participant and the truth of the statements and answers in the Proposal and Declaration of the Participant and statements of the Person Covered, shall be conditions precedent to any liability of the Takaful Fund to make any payment under this Certificate.

5. RECORDS AND REPORTS
The Participant shall keep a record of the Persons Covered under this Certificate containing, for each person, the essential particulars of the Takaful cover. The Participant shall furnish to the Takaful Operator such information concerning the persons eligible for the Takaful cover under this Certificate as may be considered to have a bearing on the administration of the Takaful cover under this Certificate and on the determination of the rates of contribution. Such records of the Persons Covered shall be open, for inspections by the Takaful Operator at any time.

6. CHANGE OF BUSINESS, OCCUPATION OR PHYSICAL CONDITION
The Participant shall give immediate notice to the Takaful Operator of any change in the Participant's address or business or occupation or the trade and shall also give notice before any renewal of the Certificate, of any injury, disease, physical defect or infirmity affecting the Person Covered.

7. PAYMENT OF TAKAFUL CONTRIBUTION
The Takaful operator shall deduct 40% (forty percent) of the Takaful Contribution shown in the Schedule that the Participant has paid based on the principle of Wakalah (Agency Fee). The balance of the Takaful Contribution shall be credited as donation into the Participant Risk Fund (PRF) (for the purpose of solidarity and cooperation among the participants) for the Takaful Operator to pay the takaful benefits to proper and rightful claimants, including the investment of the said Fund, in the manner deemed fit by the Takaful Operator in line with the approval of its Advisory Council of Experts (ACE).

8. METHOD OF SURPLUS DISTRIBUTION
Salam Takaful Ltd adopts the Selective Method of Surplus Distribution on Group Family Takaful thus; If a participant is paid a claim in respect of his insured property or interest, that participant will not be entitled to share in the participants' Surplus Distribution.

9. INVESTMENT OF THE TAKAFUL CONTRIBUTION
Salam Takaful Ltd shall manage and invest the takaful contribution paid by the Participant in conformity with the rulings of Shari‘ah as determined by ACE and other guiding laws. Any profits generated from the investments of the PRF shall be distributed as stated below:
(i) The Takaful Operator shall deduct 20% (twenty percent) of the investment return in accordance with the principle of Profit Sharing (Muḍarabah) and the balance shall be credited to the PRF.
(ii) The Takaful Operator shall deduct 20% (twenty percent) of the distributable mortality surplus of the PRF, if any in accordance with the principle of Performance Fee and in the same manner, the balance of 80% (eighty percent) shall be distributed to the Participant provided always that the Participant has not incurred any claim or received any Takaful benefits under the Certificate whilst it is in force. The said distributable mortality surplus will be determined in accordance with the followings:
-The Participants Risk Fund is not in deficit.
-It will be in accordance with the standard guidelines applicable to insurance.
-Any surplus to be distributed is recommended by the fund actuary and approved by the Takaful-Insurance Operator's Board of
Directors and the written approval of the Commission must be granted.
-Written approval of the Commission must be obtained.

10. PARTICIPATION REQUIREMENTS
In order to establish and continue this Certificate and provided the number of Members at the Commencement Date during any Certificate Year is not less than three (3) it is agreed that all present and future Eligible Employees, shall be included under the Certificate.

11. ELIGIBILITY
The Employees eligible for Takaful are the present and future Employees who are below age 65 years and are actively at work on the date they become eligible for takaful. Present Employees will be eligible as from the Commencement Date. Future employees will be eligible as from the first day of the month coincident with or from the first day of the month following their becoming an Employee.

12. EVIDENCE OF AGE
The Takaful Operator reserves the right at any time to require that the age of any Person Covered under this Certificate shall be proven to its satisfaction. Evidence of age of a Person Covered satisfactory to the Takaful Operator will be required (if not previously admitted) before any benefit in respect of him is paid under this Certificate.

13. MISSTATEMENT OF AGE
If the correct age of the Person Covered would not have been eligible for coverage under this Certificate, no benefit will be payable. The liability of the Takaful Fund shall be limited to the refund of the Takaful instalments paid without profit.

14. CLAIMS
Notice of Claim: Written notice of claim must be given to the Takaful Operator within ninety (90) days of such occurrence. Notice given by or on behalf of the Participant to the Takaful Operator with particulars sufficient to identify the Person Covered, shall be deemed to be notice to the Takaful Operator.

Examination: The Takaful Operator shall have the right and opportunity through its medical representatives to examine the Person Covered when and so often as it may reasonably require during the pendency of course of a claim hereunder and, in case of death, to investigate the circumstances of death.

Proof of Claims: All documents, information and evidences shall be furnished at the expense of the Participant and shall be in such form and such nature as the Takaful Operator may prescribe.

Payment of Claims: Any payments under this Certificate shall be payable to the Beneficiary(ies) of the Persons Covered through the Participant and the Participant shall be liable for any estate duty which may become payable in respect thereof. Any receipt or discharge which the Participant may give to the Takaful Operator for any benefit under this Certificate shall be deemed as final and complete discharge of all liability of the Takaful Fund.

15. AMOUNT OF BENEFITS AND PAYMENT OF CLAIMS
In the event of death of any Person Covered under this Certificate the Takaful Operator shall pay to the participant from the pool of contributions, upon satisfactory proof, the benefits payable under this Certificate to the Beneficiary(ies) through the Participant.

16. FREE COVER LIMIT
Evidence of health of a Person Covered satisfactory to the Takaful Operator will be required where the amount of cover in respect of the person exceeds the Free Cover Limit stated in the Schedule. Where such evidence of health is not satisfactory to the Takaful Operator, the portion of benefit which is in excess of the Free Cover Limit shall either be declined by the Takaful Operator or be granted subject to special condition as the Takaful Operator may impose.

17. ALTERATION OF CERTIFICATE
This Certificate may at any time be amended and changed, without the consent of the Person Covered hereunder or any other persons having a beneficial interest therein, upon written request being made by the Participant and agreed to by the Takaful Operator, but any amendment shall be without prejudice to any claim arising prior to the date of the change and subject to the terms and provisions the Takaful Operator may impose. No change in this Certificate shall be valid unless evidenced by an endorsement signed by an authorized officer of the Takaful Operator.

18. RENEWAL AND CANCELLATION OF CERTIFICATE
The Certificate shall not be renewable in respect of any Person Covered after the end of the period of Takaful during which such Person Covered attains the age limit as stated in the Schedule. The Takaful Operator reserves the right to vary the terms and provisions of this Certificate on any Certificate anniversary. This Certificate either in its entirety or in respect of any Person Covered may be cancelled by the Takaful Operator at any time by giving 7 (seven) days' notice by registered letter sent to the Participant at the Participant's last known address provided that such cancellation shall be without prejudice to the rights of the Participant in respect of prior injury to any Person Covered. By like notice to the Takaful Operator the Participant may at any time cancel this Certificate.

19. NO ASSIGNMENT
The benefits under this Certificate shall be non-assignable.

20. GENERAL EXCLUSIONS
Salam Takaful shall not be liable under this Certificate for Death of the Persons Covered resulted either directly or indirectly from:-
i. War, invasion, hostilities (whether war be declared or not), civil war, rebellion, revolution, insurrection, military or usurped power, riot
or civil commotion and racial strife.
ii. Death caused deliberately by a beneficiary on the certificate or a third party who has acquired interest in the Certificate.
iii. From engaging in aviation other than as a fare-paying passenger on a regular recognized air route.
iv. From the execution of a judicial sentence of death.
v. From suicide by the Person’s Covered.

21. ARBITRATION
All differences arising out of this policy shall be referred to the arbitration of some person to be appointed by both parties or, if they cannot agree upon a single Arbitrator, to the decision of two Arbitrators, one to be appointed in writing by each party; In the case of a sole arbitrator or an Umpire he/she must be knowledgeable in Takaful practices and in the case of an arbitration panel, at least one of the arbitrators must be an arbitrator knowledgeable in Takaful practices'

22. LEGAL PROCEEDINGS
No action at law or in equity, shall be brought to recover on this Certificate prior to the expiration of ninety (90) days after proof of claim has been filed in accordance with the requirements of this Certificate.

23. NO CONTRIBUTION NO COVER
Notwithstanding anything herein contained to the contrary, it is hereby declared and agreed that this Certificate is subject to the provision of Section 50(1) of the Insurance Act of 2003 that is "NO CONTRIBUTION NO COVER".

24. APPLICABLE LAW
This Certificate, and all rights, obligations and liabilities arising hereunder, shall be construed, determined and enforced in accordance with the law of Nigeria.

25. SPECIAL TAKAFUL CLAUSE
The Takaful Contribution payable under this Certificate shall be subject to the principles and practices of donation ("Tabarru"), agency with fees (Al-Wakalah Bil Ujrah), profit sharing (al-Muḍarabah) and performance fee (al-Jualah) as follows:
(i) The Participant shall agree that the agreed Takaful Contribution is made as donation to be credited into the PRF for the purpose of
solidarity and cooperation among the participants.
(ii) The Takaful Operator shall deduct 40% (forty percent) of the Takaful Contribution stated in the Schedule that the Participant has paid or undertaken to pay based on the principle agency with fees and the balance thereof shall be credited into the PRF for the Takaful Operator to manage the PRF.
(iii) The Takaful Operator shall invest the PRF in a manner deemed fit by the Takaful Operator subject to the approval of its Advisory Council of Experts (ACE) and shall deduct 20% (twenty percent) of the investment return in accordance with the principle of Muḍarabah (Profit and Loss Sharing) and the balance shall be credited to the PRF.
(iv) The Takaful Operator shall deduct 20% (twenty percent) of the distributable mortality surplus of the PRF, if any in accordance with the principle of Performance Fee and in the same manner, the balance of 80% (eighty percent) shall be distributed to the Participant provided always that the Participant has not incurred any claim or received any Takaful benefits under the Certificate whilst it is in force. The said distributable mortality surplus will be determined by independent Actuary and endorsed by the ACE of Salam Takaful.

(v) The Takaful Operator shall manage the PRF on behalf of the Participant based on the terms and conditions of the Takaful Certificate.
(vi) The distributable mortality Surplus arising from the PRF is not guaranteed and will be based on the Fund's actual experience and will first be applied to settle any loan (Qard) owing to the Takaful Operator.
(vii) In case of deficit of the PRF, the Takaful Operator shall extend a benevolent loan (Qard) to the Family Takaful and the money will be recovered by the Takaful Operator from the PRF in the subsequent year(s).
(viii) This Takaful does not cover and is not intended to cover Business, Assets, Property, Materials, Stock, Cash or any other Financial Instruments, and/or Liability or Pecuniary Interest whatsoever arising from such, of any nature, whether be it temporary or permanent, if at any time after the inception of the Certificate the Takaful Operator find such property not approved by the Takaful Operator's Advisory Council of Experts and NAICOM Takaful Advisory Council. In any such case, the Takaful Operator shall reserve the right to cancel this Certificate and refund any contribution received in respect thereof.
(ix) It is hereby declared and agreed that in line with the Shari'ah principle, the following business or occupation or trade are not covered under this Certificate:
- Pork Business
- Buildings use for prostitution Business
- Alcoholic Business
- Casino and other gambling businesses
- Any other trade or occupation not approved by the Shari'ah.

GUIDELINES AND CLAIM PROCEDURE

1. NOTICE OF CLAIM
Written notice of an occurrence upon which a claim under this certificate may be based must be given to Salam Takaful Ltd within 90 (ninety) days of such occurrence. Notice to the company must be forwarded as soon as possible although the documentation is not completed yet. Salam Takaful Ltd by its own discretion can deny the acceptance on the claim if submitted after this period and fail to give reasonable reason.

2. PROOF OF CLAIM
All documents, information and evidence required by the Company shall be furnished at the expense of the Participant and shall be in such form and such nature as the company may prescribe.

3. CLAIM FORM AND SUPPORTING DOCUMENTS
(a) completed claim form of Family Takaful Plan
(b) Original death registration certificate
(c) Original medical certificate of cause of death
(d) Police report (if death occur as result of road accident)
(e) proof of age (where the evidence of age was not previously admitted at the inception of the
certificate.
